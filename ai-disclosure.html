The short version. NativeScan's AI features send the content you ask the AI to process — typically one downscaled page image or some extracted text — through a Cloudflare proxy operated by us to Google's Gemini API for processing. AI output is generated by a third-party model and can be wrong. Use AI features only with documents you are comfortable sharing, and never rely on AI output for legal, medical, financial, or other important decisions.

1. What this Disclosure Is

This is a plain-language explanation of how NativeScan's optional AI features work and what happens to the content you submit. It is incorporated by reference into our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy and must be accepted in-app before any AI feature will function.

2. The Three AI Features

  • AutoName — generates a suggested title for a scanned document.
  • AutoSort — suggests a category (for example, Receipts, Invoices, Medical, Identity, Legal, Financial, Work, Personal) for a scanned document.
  • Recall — answers questions about your scanned documents using their extracted text content.

3. What is Transmitted, and When

None of these features run on your device. When you tap a button that triggers an AI feature, the following happens:

  1. The App prepares a payload on your device. For AutoName and AutoSort this is one page image, downscaled to at most 1024 pixels on the long side and JPEG-encoded, or a short text snippet derived from on-device OCR when image processing is not appropriate. For Recall it is the question you typed or spoke, plus the most relevant text extracted from your documents, plus a brief recent chat history.
  2. The payload is sent over HTTPS to a Cloudflare Worker operated by us at nativescan-ai-proxy.artyomlapshin1800.workers.dev. The proxy is protected by a shared secret header so requests cannot come from anywhere except the App.
  3. The proxy forwards the payload to the Google Gemini API for processing.
  4. The model's response is returned through the proxy to the App and rendered on your device.
  5. The proxy does not retain the request body or the response after the request completes. A coarse rate-limit hash derived from your IP address is kept short-term to prevent abuse and is not retained beyond the rate-limit window.

4. Third Parties Involved

  • Cloudflare operates the network on which our proxy runs. Cloudflare may process technical metadata (such as IP address) according to its own policies.
  • Google runs the Gemini API that produces the AI output. Google processes content under its API terms and Gemini data-use policies. We have configured the App to use the Gemini API rather than Google AI Studio's consumer surface; this is the path on which Google's documented terms state that user content is not used to train models.

5. What is Not Transmitted

  • Documents that you do not invoke an AI feature on remain on your device only.
  • Voice for the Recall feature is transcribed on-device by Apple's speech-recognition framework; the audio never leaves the device.
  • Your real name, email address, Apple ID, persistent user identifier, or location is not attached to any AI request by us.
  • Biometric data (Face ID / Touch ID) is handled entirely by iOS and never leaves your device.

6. Important Limitations of AI Output

  • AI can be wrong. Large language models are probabilistic. They can hallucinate, misclassify, mistranscribe, and confidently state things that are not true. You must verify any AI output before relying on it.
  • AI is not advice. AI output is not legal advice, not medical advice, not tax advice, not financial advice, and not professional advice of any kind.
  • AI output is not confidential. AI output is produced by a third-party model. We do not represent that the model's output is suitable for confidential, privileged, or regulated workflows.
  • Output may reflect bias. Models can reflect biases present in their training data. We do not warrant that AI output will be fair, balanced, complete, or appropriate for any particular use.
  • Availability is not guaranteed. AI features depend on the availability of third-party providers and may be slow, degraded, or unavailable at any time.

7. Sensitive Content

NativeScan can scan many kinds of documents, including identity documents, financial records, and medical records. Before you use an AI feature on a sensitive document, you should be comfortable with the content being processed by a third-party AI service. If you are not comfortable with that, do not invoke AI features for that document; you can categorise and name it manually. For workflows subject to legal or regulatory confidentiality requirements (for example, certain HIPAA, GDPR, or solicitor-client contexts), you should not use AI features without an independent legal review of your particular situation.

8. Your Choice

AI features are opt-in. You will be asked to accept this Disclosure the first time you try to use an AI feature. If you do not accept, AI features will not function and the App will work as a fully offline document scanner. You can change your mind by contacting nativescan.tool@gmail.com or by reinstalling the App.

9. Changes

If we change the AI provider, the data we transmit, or how we transmit it in a way that materially affects you, we will update the version number at the top of this Disclosure and require renewed in-app consent before AI features will continue to function.

One more reminder. Treat AI output as a first draft, not as a final answer. Verify anything that matters.
