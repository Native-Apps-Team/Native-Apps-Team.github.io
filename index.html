Frequently Asked Questions

How do I scan a document?

Tap the scan button on the home screen to open the camera. Point your iPhone at the document — the app will detect the edges automatically. Tap the shutter to capture, then tap Done to save.

What export formats are supported?

Free users can export as JPEG. Pro subscribers can export as PDF or DOCX. Tap the share icon on any document to choose your format.

How do I upgrade to Pro?

Tap the Pro button on the home screen or during export to view subscription options. Pro is available as a weekly or yearly subscription, with a 7-day free trial for new subscribers.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Open the Settings app on your iPhone → tap your name → Subscriptions → NativeScan → Cancel Subscription. You can also manage subscriptions directly in the App Store.

Why is the free plan limited?

The free plan includes a small number of AI analyses per month, 2 pages per scan, and JPEG export. Upgrading to Pro removes those limits and unlocks PDF and DOCX export, image adjustments, and unlimited AI.

Where are my scanned documents stored?

All scanned documents are stored locally on your iPhone. NativeScan does not keep a server-side copy. When you use AI features such as AutoName, AutoSort, or Recall, the relevant content is transmitted for processing — see our AI Use Disclosure for details.

How do I delete a document?

In the Scanned Docs list, swipe left on any document to reveal the Delete option. Tap Delete to remove it permanently from your device.

How do I delete all of my data?

Because NativeScan stores everything locally, deleting the app from your iPhone removes all scanned documents and app data. There is no server-side account to delete.

Does NativeScan use AI?

Yes. AI features (AutoName, AutoSort, Recall) are an integral part of the app. When you invoke them, the relevant content is transmitted through our secure proxy to Google's Gemini API for processing. See the AI Use Disclosure for full details, including what is and isn't transmitted.
