Plain-language summary. NativeScan stores your scanned documents only on your device. We do not maintain a server-side copy. When you use AI features such as AutoName, AutoSort, or Recall, the content you ask the AI to process is transmitted to a Cloudflare proxy operated by us and then to Google's Gemini API, processed transiently, and discarded. We do not sell your data. We do not use your data to train AI models. Free users see ads served by Google AdMob.

1. Who this Policy Applies To

This Privacy Policy describes how Artem Lapshin ("we", "us", or "our"), as the operator of the NativeScan mobile application ("App") and any related websites (collectively, the "Service"), handles information from users of the Service. It is incorporated by reference into our Terms of Service.

2. Information We Process

We have intentionally designed the App to minimise the personal information that leaves your device. The categories below describe what is processed, when, and by whom.

2.1 Stored locally on your device only

  • Scanned documents and associated metadata — captured images, edited pages, document titles, categories, custom folders, and timestamps. These remain on your device and are not transmitted to us.
  • App preferences — onboarding status, AI consent, biometric-lock setting, subscription bonus-code state, and similar small flags stored in iOS UserDefaults.
  • Biometric data — Face ID / Touch ID for app lock is handled entirely by iOS on your device. We never see, process, or transmit your biometric data.
  • Speech recognition input — voice queries for the Recall feature are transcribed by Apple's on-device speech-recognition framework. The audio does not leave your device; only the transcribed text is sent to the AI provider, and only if you have accepted the AI Use Disclosure.

2.2 Transmitted to third parties when you invoke a feature

If you have accepted the AI Use Disclosure and you invoke an AI feature, the following is transmitted from your device through our proxy to Google's Gemini API:

  • For AutoName and AutoSort: a single page of the document, downscaled to no larger than 1024 pixels on the long side and JPEG-encoded, or, when the image cannot be sent, a short text snippet derived from on-device OCR.
  • For Recall: text content extracted from your documents that is relevant to your query, plus your typed or transcribed question, plus a brief chat history if you continue a conversation.

No App account, real name, email address, or persistent user identifier is attached to those requests by us. Our proxy applies a coarse short-term rate limit using an IP-derived hash to prevent abuse; that hash is not retained.

2.3 Collected by third-party services we do not control

Some information is collected by third parties when you use the Service:

  • Apple. If you purchase a subscription, Apple processes the transaction through StoreKit. We receive only the subscription status from Apple — we do not receive your Apple ID, email address, payment method, or any direct identifier.
  • Google AdMob (free users only). AdMob may collect device identifiers (such as Apple's Identifier for Advertisers when allowed), ad-interaction data, coarse location, IP address, and similar information in order to serve and measure advertising. AdMob's processing is governed by Google's privacy policy. iOS App Tracking Transparency applies; you control tracking through your iOS Settings.
  • Cloudflare. As the operator of our AI proxy, Cloudflare may process technical information (such as IP address and request metadata) as part of providing the proxy service. Cloudflare's processing is governed by Cloudflare's privacy policy.
  • Google (Gemini API). The Gemini API processes the content you submit for an AI feature. Google's processing is governed by Google's API terms and applicable Gemini data-use policies.

We do not directly control how these third parties process information they collect from your use of their components.

3. How We Use Information

We use the limited information that reaches our infrastructure solely to:

  • operate the AI features you invoke;
  • prevent abuse of our AI proxy (rate limiting, error detection);
  • diagnose and fix bugs reported to us; and
  • comply with applicable law.

We do not:

  • sell, rent, or trade your information;
  • use your User Content to train, evaluate, or improve any AI model;
  • combine the data passing through our proxy with any other source to build a user profile;
  • send marketing emails (we do not have your email);
  • build advertising audiences from your scanned content; or
  • store scanned documents or AI request contents on our servers for any longer than is required to relay the request and the response.

6. International Transfers

We are based in Canada. When you use AI features, your content is transmitted to Cloudflare's global edge network and to Google's Gemini API, which may process the content in the United States or in other countries. By using AI features, you understand that your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own, including countries that may have different data-protection rules than those of your country.

7. Data Retention

  • On your device: data persists until you delete the document, clear app data, or uninstall the App.
  • In our proxy: request contents are not persisted by us. Transient operational logs (timestamps, error codes, an IP-derived rate-limit hash) are kept only as long as needed to operate and secure the service, and are not used to identify individual users.
  • Third parties retain information according to their own retention policies, which we do not control.

8. Security

We use commercially reasonable security measures, including HTTPS in transit, a shared-secret header between the App and our proxy, and short-term rate limiting. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, however, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. Because documents are stored on your device, the security of those documents depends primarily on the security of your device, your passcode, and your iCloud configuration. We strongly recommend enabling a device passcode and the in-app Face ID / Touch ID lock.
